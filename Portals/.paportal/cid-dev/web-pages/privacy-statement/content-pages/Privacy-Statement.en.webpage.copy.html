Transport Canada (TC) is committed to protecting the privacy rights of individuals and safeguarding the confidentiality of information under its control provided by individuals and institutions, in accordance with the law. “Personal information” is defined as any information, in any form, about an identifiable individual (e.g. contact details); refer to section 3 of the Privacy Act for further details regarding personal information.

Personal information collected by TC is protected from disclosure to unauthorized persons and/or agencies subject to the provisions of the Privacy Act. Individuals have the right to the protection of and access to their personal information under the Privacy Act, subject to certain exceptions and exemptions, and to request corrections where the individual believes there is an error or omission. Individuals may contact the Department's Access to Information and Privacy Protection Division to request access or correction of their personal information.

The personal information is collected under the authority of the Transportation of Dangerous Goods (TDG) Act, 1992 (the Act). Personal information may be shared internally at TC for the purpose of enforcing the Act.

Personal information is collected for the purpose of registering a person (individual or an organization) with Transport Canada that imports, offers for transport, handles or transports dangerous goods in Canada, to ensure compliance to the Transportation of Dangerous Goods (TDG) Act, 1992. 

Your personal information will be kept for the duration of your registration with Transport Canada and for ten years after the end of the registration.

Your personal information will be protected under the provisions of the Privacy Act and a description can be found in the TC Personal Information Bank, TC TDG 001. Detailed information can be found under the TC Info Source Chapter, a copy of which is available in major public and academic libraries or online at www.infosource.gc.ca. Individuals have the right to complain to the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada regarding the institution's handling of their personal information.

Failure to register a person (individual or an organization) with Transport Canada that imports, offers for transport, handles or transports dangerous goods in Canada, may result in fines or prosecution for those responsible.
